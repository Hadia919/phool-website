Surgery: A Complete Student Guide

Author: Abdul Rehman Aydan

Chapter 1: A Curious Beginning

I have always been someone who wants to understand how things work. I am 15 years old and I study at Allied School, a project of the Punjab Group of Colleges. My main subject is computer science, a field that trains my mind to think logically and solve problems step by step. Biology has become a strong personal interest.

Chapter 2: Early Civilizations and the First Surgical Efforts

Early civilizations worked with limited tools and little knowledge, yet they still tried to heal. Egyptian records show wound cleaning, bone setting and stitching. In India, Sushruta described hundreds of procedures.

Chapter 3: The Middle Ages and the Slowing of Progress

During the Middle Ages, surgery advanced slowly. Barber-surgeons treated wounds and preserved early surgical methods.

Chapter 4: The Scientific Turning Point

The 17th and 18th centuries brought scientific change. Anatomy improved and hospitals became learning centers.

Chapter 5: Anesthesia and Antisepsis

Anesthesia allowed painless surgery. Antiseptic methods reduced infection and improved safety.

Chapter 6: Growth of Surgical Specialties

Surgery expanded into neurosurgery, cardiac surgery, orthopedic surgery and more.

Chapter 7: How Medical Universities Work

Medical universities teach foundational sciences and clinical practice through structured training.

Chapter 8: The Road to Becoming a Surgeon

Graduates enter residency programs to gain hands-on surgical experience.

Chapter 9: First Aid Every Student Should Know

First aid includes cleaning wounds, applying pressure and preventing further harm.

Chapter 10: The Future of Surgery

Technology such as robotics and imaging continues to shape modern surgery.
